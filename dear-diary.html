Deri Kochoha Ethiopia

Farm: Deri Kochoha

Producer: 600 small holders

Region: Sidamo, Ethiopia

Elevation: 1800-2000 masl*

Variety: Heirloom

Processing Method: Washed

Harvest Period: July & August 2015

* Meters Above Sea Level

This coffee is from the town of Hagere Mariam in the Guji Zone, located within Sidamo. Deri Kochoha is a washing station where about 600 small holders bring their coffee in cherry for processing. They use a disc pulper to remove cherry skins, and a traditional 72 hour fermentation to break down the mucilage.

The coffee is vigorously agitated with wooden paddles during fermentation, and washed over night in a clean water bath to fully remove the mucilage. Finally, it is dried on raised beds.

Because this coffee was purchased through the Ethiopia Coffee Exchange (ECX), it is difficult to know too much about the daily lives of the producers from who’s farm this coffee came. Instead, we chose this coffee for its complexity, nuanced flavors, and incredible body. It has a beautiful honey-like mouthfeel with peach, apricot, and meyer lemon notes, paired with a clean, black tea finish.

Gachatha Kenya

Farm: Gachatha

Producer: 900 small holders

Region: Nyeri

Elevation: 1800-2100 masl*

Variety: SL-28, Riuru 11

Processing Method: Washed

Harvest Period: August 2015

* Meters Above Sea Level

The Gachatha Coffee Factory rests between Muthuaini, Thiriku, Gachenge, and Kianjau, and was built in 1963 to form the Gachatha Farmers Co-operative Society Ltd. There are currently 900 active farmers and over 1,000 members, supported by nine permanent staff and 25 seasonal workers. Permanent staff are split between the key roles of coffee weighing and grading at reception, ensuring farmer payment, and addressing farmers’ complaints while seasonal staff help with cherry reception, depulping, and drying.

The surrounding Gachatha area is densely populated, so every effort is made to minimize impact. The factory treats all water in soak pits to ensure no contaminates run into the local waterways, which are a source for drinking water. The community also places great importance on protecting indigenous trees in the area, so that local bird life can be sustained.

We fell in love with this coffee both for the Co-op’s social and environmental sustainability focus, and for its lovely taste. It is big, juicy, and sweet, with notes of black cherry, plums, and marzipan. It has a complex, well defined acidity and great structure.
